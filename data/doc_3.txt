Vector Embeddings and Vector Databases

        Vector embeddings are numerical representations of data, such as text, images, or audio, captured in a high-dimensional continuous vector space. These dense vectors are generated by deep learning models to capture the semantic and contextual meaning of words or phrases rather than just their literal characters.
        In an embedding space, words with similar meanings or contexts are mathematically positioned closer to one another, allowing computers to compute contextual similarity.
        Vector databases are specialized storage engines designed specifically to index, store, and rapidly query high-dimensional vectors. Traditional relational databases struggle with vector data because standard indexing methods cannot scale to high dimensions.
        Vector databases utilize advanced indexing algorithms like Hierarchical Navigable Small World (HNSW) or Inverted File Index (IVF) to perform Approximate Nearest Neighbor (ANN) searches, allowing systems to look through millions of vectors in milliseconds.
