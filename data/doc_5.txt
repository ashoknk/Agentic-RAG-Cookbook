Large Language Models (LLMs) and Fine-Tuning

        Large Language Models (LLMs) are massive deep learning models trained on trillions of tokens of text data to predict the next word in a sequence. Built predominantly on the Transformer architecture, these models use billions of parameters to learn the syntax, semantics, and nuances of human language.
        While foundational models exhibit impressive general capabilities, they often require domain-specific adaptation to perform specialized tasks accurately.
        Fine-tuning is the process of taking a pre-trained foundational model and training it further on a smaller, targeted dataset to adapt it to a specific task or industry. Full parameter fine-tuning updates all weights within the network but requires immense computational power.
        To mitigate this, developers use Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) techniques like LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation), which freeze the original model weights and inject small, trainable rank-decomposition matrices into the network layers, dramatically reducing training costs and hardware requirements.
